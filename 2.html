What to Expect in a Good Quality T-Shirt

JUNE 15, 2024

A T-shirt is a staple in every wardrobe, but not all T-shirts are created equal. When investing in custom apparel or choosing a blank for your brand, understanding what defines "quality" is essential for durability and comfort.

1. Fabric Composition and Feel

Quality begins with the yarn. Look for 100% Combed and Ring-Spun Cotton. Unlike regular cotton, the combing process removes short fibers and impurities, resulting in a smoother, stronger fabric that feels soft against the skin and provides a superior surface for printing.

2. Stitching and Construction

The durability of a garment is hidden in the seams. A high-quality T-shirt features:

  • Side-Seamed Construction: Provides a more tailored, retail fit compared to tubular shirts.
  • Double-Needle Stitching: Usually found on the sleeves and bottom hem to prevent unraveling.
  • Shoulder-to-Shoulder Taping: Reinforces the neck and shoulder seams for better shape retention.

3. Consistent Fit

A premium T-shirt should maintain its proportions. It should not "torque" (twist) after the first wash. Quality manufacturers pre-shrunk their fabrics to ensure that the size you buy is the size that stays.

4. Color Fastness

High-quality dyes ensure that colors remain vibrant even after multiple washes. For custom prints, a quality base garment ensures that the ink bonds correctly, preventing cracking or premature fading of the design.

"Quality is not an act, it is a habit. In the world of apparel, it is the difference between a garment you wear once and a garment you keep for years."

Whether you are ordering for a corporate event or launching your own clothing line, these benchmarks will help you choose products that reflect excellence.
